F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions About Sri Lankan Tea

What makes Sri Lankan tea (Ceylon tea) unique?

Sri Lankan tea is renowned for its rich flavor, fragrance, and high quality. The diversity of elevations, climates, and soil types across the country produces a range of distinct flavors, from light and citrusy to bold and full-bodied.

What are the main tea-growing regions in Sri Lanka?

The seven primary tea-growing regions are Nuwara Eliya, Uva, Uda Pussellawa, Dimbula, Kandy, Ruhuna, and Sabaragamuwa. Each region produces tea with distinct flavor profiles influenced by altitude and climate.

Is all Sri Lankan tea handpicked?

Yes, most Sri Lankan tea is handpicked to ensure only the tender two leaves and a bud are selected. This traditional method contributes to the superior quality of Ceylon tea.

What types of tea are produced in Sri Lanka?

Sri Lanka produces mainly black tea, but also green tea and white tea in smaller quantities. Each type has its own characteristics and health benefits.

How is Sri Lankan tea processed?

The tea leaves undergo several steps: withering, rolling, fermentation (for black tea), drying, and grading. These processes are carried out using traditional techniques and modern quality standards.

Can I visit tea plantations in Sri Lanka?

Yes! Many tea estates in Sri Lanka, especially in the central highlands, offer guided tours, factory visits, and tastings. It’s a great way to experience the full journey from leaf to cup.
